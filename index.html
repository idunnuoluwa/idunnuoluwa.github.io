About

Idunnuoluwa A. Adeniji is a PhD student in Computer Science at the University of Illinois Chicago, conducting research at the Argonne Leadership Computing Facility (ALCF), Argonne National Laboratory. Her research sits at the intersection of GPU-accelerated scientific visualization, human-computer interaction, and high-performance computing.

Her latest project is OVA (OpenCosmo Visualization Application) — a fully client-side, WebGPU-accelerated 3D particle viewer embedded in the OpenCosmo compute portal at ALCF. OVA's rendering pipeline was migrated from WebGL2 to WebGPU, moving per-particle colormapping, filtering, and level-of-detail decimation onto GPU compute shaders. On commodity laptop hardware, WebGPU sustains 120 FPS through 82.6M cosmological particles while using roughly half the heap of the WebGL implementation — extending the interactive ceiling well beyond what browser-based rendering has previously achieved without dedicated visualization infrastructure.

She holds a B.Sc. in Computational Science and Engineering from Kean University, graduating summa cum laude with Honors Distinction in Research. She has collaborated with the ALCF Visualization Team across multiple research appointments, with work spanning browser-based GPU rendering, immersive XR environments, and large-scale scientific data exploration. Her publications appear at IEEE LDAV, IEEE eScience, and the Journal of Computational Science Education.

Beyond research, Idunnuoluwa is drawn to the boundary between computation and experience — particularly how real-time rendering and immersive technologies can make scientific data not just interpretable, but compelling. This conviction shapes her approach to visualization: the tools we build for science should be as engaging as they are functional.
